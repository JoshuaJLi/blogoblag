Some Stuff I Liked Last Year!

January 9, 2023

It's the end of the year! So that means I can look back on all the media I consumed this year and give a shoutout to anything I liked. It's not for things that came out this year, just things that I consumed this year. Did I get this done late and it's January already? Yes!

Dropout

I originally subscribed to Dropout because I wanted to watch Game Changer and their new Make Some Noise, but oh boy there is so much content coming from there it has changed the way I consume internet content. Turns out the combination of internet humor and gameshows is exactly up my alley. And it's fun looking through the backlog and seeing some old CollegeHumor stuff that I must have seen 10 years ago. And who knew, Dimension 20 is really good. And I really see the value in having everything release on a schedule one week at a time, the drip feed of content really does make things better. And it's always fun to remember that this Tuesday is a new Dirty Laundry, or that tomorrow there's a new Play It By Ear. Having a set amount of content being released on a consistent basis has been great and its helped me move away from mindlessly watching Youtube or browsing Reddit all the time.

There's so much I haven't even touched yet, but here's what I watched and enjoyed

  • Dirty Laundry - If you've watched Would I Lie to You? (or any of its clips) its like that but not as British. And the cocktail tutorials are fascinating to watch as someone with no interest in them.
  • Game Changer (and Make Some Noise) - I don't even know what I can say about this show that hasn't already been said. It's great. And the 5th season that's currently coming out is already great and its not even finished yet.
  • Dimension 20: A Court of Fey and Flowers - I wasn't planning on jumping into Dimension 20 because watching 2 hour episodes every week felt like crazy talk. But then I got covid and thought "well I got nothing else to do let's watch the new D20 season" and boy howdy. I wasn't one for regency dramas, or so I thought, but turns out you can hide it under a veneer of DnD and I will be right into it. I don't remember the last time I was this invested in a cast of characters. Everyone does amazing and I could go on all day without properly describing how I like it so much. You gotta try this one out, at least finish the second episode before you think its not for you. And all the fanart? amazing.

Triple Click

I hadn't had a gaming podcast in my rotation for a couple of years until about last year when The Besties came back, and from that return I heard Triple Click's Kirk Hamilton guest for one episode and decided to give it a shot. It's a great gamer (lowercase g) podcast with a good mix of actual discussion about video games and lighthearted humor. You never know what you'll get each week, whether it be a review of a game, a weekly news roundup, or a deep dive into some general aspect of video games. Shoutouts to Kirk's ECOTAG system for categorizing open world games, that was the episode I started listening and it was a great into to the podcast and a great system, I think about it every time I look at an open world game. Maybe I haven't been keeping up with games as much but I feel like I learn something new from them every week. It's definitely my best podcast find of the year.

Dungeons and Daddies Patreon

Combining subscription-based entertainment and podcasts from the two above posts, all the content I get for ~$7CAD a month from Dungeons and Daddies (not a bdsm podcast) has been well worth my dollars. Teen Talk is a great episode meta-discussion that provides behind the scenes of a dnd podcast and also having "two dudes talking" energy but in a good way. And the Sons and Sonsability miniseries was also some of the funniest audio content I have listened to this year. (Good year for regency-era dnd content). Plus its great to not have ads, its not something I realized would be nice until I had it.

Sacrifical Trash

Some comment on the video that I can no longer find put it pretty succintly as (paraphrasing) "distilling thoughts I had had on the topic for a while into an understanable work" which is exactly how I would describe this video essay. Before I just had a cloud of ideas floating in my head but this video helped cool it down, condense onto a surface, and become collected into a bottle in the form of a liquid. I've always been wary of internet hate mobs for media and this video has been a good way to express how I feel about it. I want to show this video to everyone, hence why its here.

Outer Wilds

I have complicated feelings about this game because it took me a good 8 months to complete, but sometimes the longer something takes the better the payoff. That usually isn't true don't fall for the sunk cost fallacy, cut your losses while you can. Anyway, finishing this game was probably one of my best video game experiences just for all the emotions I felt. While playing I was thinking "I hate this I hate this I'm so confused" but looking back, I'm thinking "damn that was a really good game and I wish I could experience that all again." It's a beautiful, confusing game. Last thing, DO NOT LOOK ANYTHING UP ABOUT THIS GAME. Looking back I could have figured out everything I was stuck on. If you really get stuck, ask someone who finished the game for vague hints, so much of the joy of the game is learning yourself.

Divinity Original Sin 2

Speaking of games that I took forever to complete, I started this one Summer 2020 and only finished it this Spring. It took me a bit to really get into the meat of it, but once it started clicking for me, it really clicked. One of the things that made me bounce off of the game was some of the super difficult random encounters that would wipe the team out of nowhere. But once I learned the art of same scumming, I was invincible. Being able to just avoid that encounter aside, it also allowed me to make a pseudo rewind system that allowed me to try out new mechanics and learn how to play the game. Once I got over the curve I really enjoyed all the strategy that comes into the combat and it really helped me enjoy the game. Might make me want to get into more turn based strategy games.
